1. Lynn Creek placers (Mindat locality ID 60622) – Lynn Mining District, Eureka County, Nevada, USA
Location and basic data
Locality name: Lynn Creek placers – part of the Lynn Mining District in Eureka County, Nevada, USA.
Type of locality: This locality is classified as a group of placers.
Coordinates:
WGS 84 (degrees, minutes, seconds): 40° 55′ 32″ North, 116° 17′ 20″ West.
Decimal degrees: 40.92575 N, ‑116.28897 W.
Climate: The Köppen climate type is BSk (cold semi‑arid/steppe).
Nearest settlements: The nearest town listed is Carlin (population 2,302 in 2017) located about 28.2 km away.
Identifiers:
Mindat locality ID: 60622.
Long‑form identifier: mindat:1:2:60622:3.
GUID: 0.
Deposit description and geology
The deposit is a channel extending eastward from the lode mining area for about 4 miles. In the upper 2.5 miles the gravels are ~25 ft wide and 10 to 28 ft deep, and the pay streak (productive alluvial layer) is the first 1.5 ft of alluvium lying on bedrock.
Workings: numerous cuts, pits, trenches and shafts.
Reserves: Indicated reserves exceed 1 million cubic yards.
History: The channel was being worked by the Ura Gold Mines Co. in 1952.
Deposit type: Placer Au‑PGE (placer deposit containing gold and platinum‑group elements).
Geology: Gold is probably derived from mineralized shear zones in chert and shale of the Vinini Formation. The gold recovered is very fine, averaging 920 to 960 fine.
Rock formation(s): The placer deposit is hosted in stream gravels.

2. Adelaide Mine (Mindat locality ID 59702) – Gold Run Mining District, Humboldt County, Nevada, USA
Location and basic data
Locality name: Adelaide mine – part of the Gold Run Mining District in Humboldt County, Nevada, USA.
Type of locality: Mine.
Coordinates:
WGS 84 (degrees, minutes, seconds): 40° 48′ 18″ North, 117° 29′ 40″ West.
Decimal degrees: 40.80509 N, ‑117.49462 W.
Climate: Köppen type BSk (cold semi‑arid/steppe).
Nearest settlements:
Golconda – population 214 (2011) – 16.5 km away.
Winnemucca – population 7,887 (2017) – 27.5 km away.
Identifiers:
Mindat locality ID: 59702.
Long‑form identifier: mindat:1:2:59702:2.
GUID: 0.
Other/historical names: Nbmg Sample Site 2301 and Sec 20 T34N R40E.
Structural setting: The area is affected by NE‑trending folds and faults.
Alteration: Oxidation.
Deposit description and history
Deposit type: Skarn Cu.
Deposit description: It is a sulfide replacement of marble and calcareous siltstone. Replacement bodies appear to follow “transposed bedding” (isoclinally folded rock) with north‑trending high‑angle faults and low‑angle thrust faults influencing localization. Rocks east of the replaced units are hard, laminated sandstone and siltstone. Carbonate‑bearing rocks are converted to calcsilicate rocks with light brown garnet, and vesuvianite and scheelite are reported in mine waste dumps.
History and development:
Discovery: 1860s.
The mine operated from the 1860s through 1940, producing the major portion of the gold, silver, copper and lead for the district.
Production resumed in 1980 when Bullion Monarch Co. owned a 50 % interest in M.& B. Mining Co.; by October 1980 about 1,000 tons of ore had been mined and stockpiled for delivery to Monarch’s 200‑ton‑per‑day flotation mill at Austin.
The property was inactive in 1984 according to Garside.
Geology and rock formations
Ore minerals: The ore consists mainly of chalcopyrite, pyrrhotite and minor sphalerite and galena in a gangue of calcite, garnet and vesuvianite.
Replacement bodies: These replacement‑type bodies occur in limestone interbedded with dark calcareous phyllite.
Scheelite traces: Scheelite is reported in the mine waste dumps.
Rock formation(s): Listed as the Preble Formation (duplicated entry suggests both the host and footwall are Preble Formation).
Ore structural control: The ore occurs in a fault zone.

Commodity list: The exploitable commodities recorded at this locality are copper, gold, lead, silver, tungsten and zinc.
